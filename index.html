Part 3: Two Races — Wheat and Tares (Matthew 13)

Jesus taught in parables to reveal spiritual truth to those with ears to hear. In Matthew 13, He reveals a hidden truth: two spiritual lineages exist on earth — one from God (wheat) and one sown by the enemy (tares). They grow together until the harvest — the end of the world.

These two types look similar outwardly, but their fruit — their actions — prove their source. This aligns with the mystery of Cain and Abel. One was righteous, the other not. Jesus said, “By their fruits ye shall know them” (Matthew 7:20). Ask: Do they lie? Do they honor Christ?

Matthew 13:24–25
"The kingdom of heaven is likened unto a man which sowed good seed in his field: But while men slept, his enemy came and sowed tares among the wheat, and went his way." 🎧 Listen to Matthew 13
Matthew 13:30
"Let both grow together until the harvest: and in the time of harvest I will say to the reapers, Gather ye together first the tares, and bind them in bundles to burn them: but gather the wheat into my barn." 🎧 Listen to Matthew 13

Part 5: Genesis and the Mystery of Elohim

The book of Genesis reveals something hidden in plain sight. In Genesis 1, the Hebrew word used for “God” is “Elohim” (H430) — a plural term meaning “gods, angels, magistrates.” The “LORD God” (YHWH Elohim), the personal covenant name of the Most High, doesn’t appear until Genesis 2. This distinction is deliberate.

Many believe Genesis 1 refers to the Supreme God, but the text suggests it speaks of delegated beings — Elohim. God allows His angels access to His power, and they can create under His authority. But not all remained loyal. Lucifer and the fallen made their own system — this world — as a counterfeit to God’s design.

Genesis 1:1
"In the beginning God (Elohim) created the heaven and the earth." 🎧 Listen to Genesis 1

When Elohim created man in their image, the word “image” (H6754) implies a shadow or phantom — an idol, a vain show. But the LORD God (YHWH Elohim) did not form man with a soul until Genesis 2.

Genesis 1:27
"So God (Elohim) created man in his own image... male and female created he them." 🎧 Listen to Genesis 1
Genesis 2:2
"And on the seventh day God (Elohim) ended his work (deputyship H4399) which he had made; and he rested..." 🎧 Listen to Genesis 2

When the “LORD God” forms man from the dust in Genesis 2:7, something changes — He breathes life into man and gives him a living soul. This is where true spiritual identity begins — not just a vessel made in the shadow of something greater, but a soul with divine breath.

Genesis 2:7
"And the LORD God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living soul." 🎧 Listen to Genesis 2

Part 6: El, Immanuel, and the Name of God

Many names in the Bible carry God’s identity. The word “El” (H410) is a name for the Most High — the Almighty God. It’s embedded in names like Emmanu-EL (God with us), Dani-EL (God is my judge), and Micha-EL (Who is like God?). These names testify to the LORD’s presence and purpose.

When Isaiah prophesied the virgin birth of Jesus, he said His name would be Immanuel. This means “God with us.” In the Gospel of Matthew, this prophecy is fulfilled — God came in the flesh. Christ is not just a teacher or prophet — He is El, the Almighty, walking among us.

Isaiah 7:14
"Therefore the Lord himself shall give you a sign; Behold, a virgin shall conceive, and bear a son, and shall call his name Immanuel." 🎧 Listen to Isaiah 7
Matthew 1:23
"Behold, a virgin shall be with child, and shall bring forth a son, and they shall call his name Emmanuel, which being interpreted is, God with us." 🎧 Listen to Matthew 1

To know His name is to know His nature. He is the same LORD God who revealed Himself to Moses at the burning bush as I AM (Exodus 3:14), and who now calls us to Himself through Christ. Jesus said, “Before Abraham was, I am” (John 8:58), proving that He is one with El — eternal and uncreated.

John 8:58
"Jesus said unto them, Verily, verily, I say unto you, Before Abraham was, I am." 🎧 Listen to John 8
