Part 1: Fundamentals and Scripture

This world is inverted. People are so programmed from birth that most ignore the Bible fundamentals. But it’s not simply ignorance — the truth is only revealed by the LORD. As Jesus told Peter, "flesh and blood hath not revealed it unto thee, but my Father which is in heaven" (Matthew 16:17). Only the LORD can open eyes and hearts to see who Christ truly is. Jesus Christ is the way, the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father but through Him (John 14:6). Daniel 2 reminds us that God is the revealer of secrets — and He is lifting the veil in these last days.

Part 2: The Devil is a Liar — and Runs the Kingdoms of the World

The devil is the father of lies and has been a deceiver from the beginning. He offered Jesus all the kingdoms of the world because they are under his control — for now. Jesus rejected him, revealing the truth. Most of the world is blind to this reality because God has not revealed it to them yet. As Jesus said to Peter, "flesh and blood hath not revealed it unto thee, but my Father which is in heaven" (Matthew 16:17). Paul also wrote, "the god of this world hath blinded the minds of them which believe not" (2 Corinthians 4:4). Only the LORD can open spiritual eyes.

Part 3: Two Races — Wheat and Tares (Matthew 13)

Jesus taught in parables to reveal spiritual truth to those with ears to hear. In Matthew 13, He reveals a hidden truth: two spiritual lineages exist on earth — one from God (wheat) and one sown by the enemy (tares). They grow together until the harvest — the end of the world.

These two types look similar outwardly, but their fruit — their actions — prove their source. This aligns with the mystery of Cain and Abel. One was righteous, the other not. Jesus said, “By their fruits ye shall know them” (Matthew 7:20). Ask: Do they lie? Do they honor Christ?

Matthew 13:24–25
"The kingdom of heaven is likened unto a man which sowed good seed in his field: But while men slept, his enemy came and sowed tares among the wheat, and went his way." 🎧 Listen to Matthew 13
Matthew 13:30
"Let both grow together until the harvest: and in the time of harvest I will say to the reapers, Gather ye together first the tares, and bind them in bundles to burn them: but gather the wheat into my barn." 🎧 Listen to Matthew 13
